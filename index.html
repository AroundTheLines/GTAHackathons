Hackathons in the GTA

GTAHackathons is where you can find all the events to satisfy your hackathon needs,
right in the greater Toronto area.

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where participants can work on any software and hardware project of their choosing without distraction. These events range from 36 to 54 hours on average, with most being 36 hours long. At the end of the competition you will be given the chance to present your project to the audience for a chance at winning one of the various prizes. There will be mentors and volunteers to assist you at every step of the way.

Who can come?

That depends on the event. Some hackathons are specifically for students in university or high school, while others are open to anyone who applies and is accepted. They do share one thing in common though, which is that anyone who's interested in technology and willing to learn will fit right in.

But what if I'm inexperienced?

No worries! So long as you're willing to learn you'll be a great fit at a hackathon. They're great ways to get the experience that you're missing.

Do I have to do this alone?

No way! The majority of hackathons allow for teams of up to 4 people, somtimes more. Hacking with peers is where the fun happens. If you don't have friends to team up with beforehand, most hackathons have a period of time alotted before the hacking happens for people to find teams. Make sure you check the schedules of every individual hackathon to make sure though!

What should I bring?

Your laptop and and accessories, anything you want to hack with, a water bottle, a change of clothing, and a sleeping bag (if you don't have accomodations) are the necessities. It'd also be a good idea to bring along a sweater and some extra charging cords for any electronics that you bring as well. Write down whatever you bring on a list before the hackathon so that you don't lose anything later.
